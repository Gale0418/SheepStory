# Worked Example: Fake Genius → Reasoning Chain

## Bad Pattern

```text
He smiled. He had already seen through the enemy's plan.
```

## Why It Fails

- intelligence is asserted, not demonstrated
- no clue is visible
- opponent competence disappears
- reader cannot reconstruct the logic

## Better Direction

Show observation, option rejection, and tradeoff.

```text
The convoy did not slow at the broken bridge.

That was the mistake. Not the bridge—the confidence.

If they had scouts in the ravine, they would have slowed. If they had aerial cover, they would have spread out. They did neither, which meant they had received a clean road report from someone they trusted.

So the leak was not in his camp.

It was in theirs.
```

## What Improved

- specific clue
- reasoning chain
- alternatives rejected
- conclusion is earned

## Checklist

- [ ] What did the character notice?
- [ ] What alternatives were ruled out?
- [ ] Why is the opponent still competent?
