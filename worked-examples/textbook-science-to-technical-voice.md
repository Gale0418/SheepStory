# Worked Example: Textbook Science → Technical Voice

## Bad Pattern

```text
Steel has a melting point, and different alloys behave differently at high temperatures. This is important in engineering.
```

## Why It Fails

- true but inert
- no immediate problem
- no decision changes
- no character voice

## Better Direction

Make the explanation solve a scene problem.

```text
The patch did not need to be pretty. It needed to fail after the frame, not before it.

I checked the numbers twice because dying from arithmetic would be embarrassing. The frame alloy would soften first. My patch had a higher margin, even if it looked thinner and cheaper and generally like something a desperate person had cut out with bad tools.

Good enough. Which, in engineering, is the formal term for please do not explode yet.
```

## What Improved

- technical detail changes action
- uncertainty remains
- humor reveals fear
- voice belongs to a person under pressure

## Checklist

- [ ] What problem caused the explanation?
- [ ] What decision changed?
- [ ] What human reaction leaks through?
