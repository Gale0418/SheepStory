# Worked Example: Polite Dialogue → Subtext

## Bad Pattern

```text
“I am upset because you lied to me.”
“I understand. I should have told you the truth.”
“I forgive you.”
```

## Why It Fails

- conflict resolves too quickly
- everyone names feelings directly
- no obstacle under the lines
- no character-specific voice

## Better Direction

Let the real subject hide under a smaller subject.

```text
“You changed the door code.”

“You noticed.”

“I noticed when I stood outside for six minutes with groceries melting through the bag.”

“It was temporary.”

“So was trusting you, apparently.”
```

## What Improved

- betrayal appears through a concrete object
- the conflict remains active
- subtext is stronger than direct explanation
- the scene creates next pressure instead of closing it

## Checklist

- [ ] What does each speaker want?
- [ ] What do they refuse to say?
- [ ] What object carries the conflict?
