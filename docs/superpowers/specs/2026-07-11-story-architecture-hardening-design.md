# Story Architecture Hardening Design

## Goal

Turn useful short-fiction prompt ideas into reusable SheepStory contracts without making four-act structure, sympathetic villains, fixed word-count beats, or project-specific lore universal defaults.

## Design Principles

- Preserve proportional Quick, Standard, and Longform workflows.
- Treat structure as a selectable model, not a mandatory formula.
- Turn character limits, opposition logic, promises, and endings into inspectable obligations.
- Keep project-specific requirements in a Project Brief; never promote them to generic canon.
- Prefer causal evidence over numeric quality proxies such as a climax every N words or a fixed payoff percentage.
- Reuse existing scene-texture, genius-strategy, continuity, and chapter-contract guidance instead of duplicating it.

## Components

### Project Brief

Add a project-level template for format, target scale, required and forbidden elements, immutable character truths, capability ceilings, world constraints, content boundaries, and still-open choices. It sits above the Story Bible and records user requirements without pretending proposals are approved canon.

### Story Architecture

Add a planning reference used for complete short stories, multi-chapter arcs, and ending-sensitive work. It selects the lightest useful structure from causal, four-act pressure, rise/fall, episodic, or mosaic models, then records opening condition, escalating choices, crisis, climax decision, consequence, core-question answer, intentionally open threads, and optional opening-ending resonance.

Four-act structure remains available when pressure escalation fits the story. Word ranges and recurring-climax quotas remain project choices, not skill rules.

### Opposition Design

Add a reference that handles people, institutions, environments, relationships, and internal opposition. Opposition must have a goal, justification, stake, resources, constraints, competence, blind spot, unacceptable cost, and independent action. It may be understandable without being sympathetic or redeemable.

### Character and Capability Limits

Extend Character Cards with a flaw/coping strategy and an explicit capability ceiling. Solutions must be reconstructable from approved skills, resources, time, information, and costs; new exceptional ability requires canon approval.

### Promise Lifecycle and Ending Contract

Add a promise template with setup evidence, reader and character knowledge, intended payoff window, permitted transformations, consequence, closure evidence, and lifecycle states. Extend chapter contracts with relevant promise changes and an optional ending contract for core-question answer, climax choice, price, consequence, open threads, and resonance.

## Non-Goals

- No mandatory four-act outline.
- No mandatory sympathetic antagonist.
- No fixed dialogue percentage, beat interval, word allocation, or payoff-rate KPI.
- No Taipei, lost-technology, childhood-friend, or other example-specific defaults.
- No automatic memory writes, plugin installation, commit, or publication.

## Verification

- Static checks require all new references, templates, and acceptance specifications.
- Regression checks require routing, structure-choice language, project-specific constraint isolation, opposition coherence, capability ceilings, promise lifecycle states, and ending-contract fields.
- Behavioral specifications cover optional structure choice, non-caricature opposition, bounded protagonist competence, promise payoff, and project-only identity constraints.
- Existing validators and genericity scans must continue to pass.
