# Style Profile: zh-TW Fiction

## Purpose

Write and revise fiction in natural Taiwan Traditional Chinese (`zh-TW`) when the user requests Taiwanese usage or the project declares this profile.

## Language Defaults

- Use Traditional Chinese characters and Taiwan-preferred vocabulary unless character voice or setting requires another regional form.
- Use full-width Chinese punctuation in Chinese prose; preserve deliberate stylistic exceptions.
- Let particles, contractions, slang, code-switching, and profanity follow character background rather than one universal register.
- Prefer context-appropriate pronouns and omitted subjects over mechanically repeating names or pronouns.
- Keep dialogue speakable. Read for breath, interruption, hesitation, and what a Taiwanese speaker would naturally leave unsaid.

## Avoid Translation-Like Chinese

Check for翻譯腔與不必要的英文直譯：

- excessive explicit subjects where Chinese would omit them
- abstract noun stacks replacing concrete verbs
- repeated 「這意味著」「事實是」「在某種程度上」 without character purpose
- English connective logic copied into every sentence
- unnatural passive constructions or possessives
- uniformly complete, polite, grammatically symmetrical dialogue

Do not delete a construction merely because it resembles another language. Preserve it when it belongs to a character, narrator, institution, translation device, or deliberate alienation effect.

## Fiction Checks

- Distinguish narration register from each character's spoken register.
- Preserve ambiguity that Chinese syntax carries naturally.
- Use idioms and four-character phrases only when they fit narrator and era; do not decorate every paragraph with them.
- Keep sensory detail concrete and locally legible without turning Taiwan usage into stereotype.
- When converting Simplified Chinese or another locale, adapt vocabulary and rhythm as well as characters.
- Preserve names, invented terminology, and established project spellings unless the user requests localization.

## Revision Pass

1. Mark phrases that sound translated, bureaucratic, or assistant-like.
2. Identify whether each is intentional voice or accidental drift.
3. Replace accidental drift with a shorter verb-led or image-led construction.
4. Re-read dialogue for breath and speaker identity.
5. Confirm the revision did not erase roughness, dialect, or project-specific terminology.

## Boundary

This profile defines locale behavior, not genre. It does not automatically add light-novel phrasing, literary lyricism, Taiwanese settings, dialect, comedy, or hard-SF texture.
