# Anti-AI Flavour

## Purpose

This reference removes AI flavour while preserving authorial voice. The goal is not to make prose smooth. The goal is to make prose feel chosen.

Use this file to detect and correct plastic or templated symptoms. For general requests such as natural, concise, plain-language, conversational, or voice-preserving rewriting, use `editorial-rewrite.md` for the transformation contract and mode selection.

## AI Flavour Symptoms

Common symptoms:

- generic emotional summary
- over-explaining motives
- symmetrical paragraph structure
- every list has three items
- every section has the same rhythm
- motivational-poster sentences
- vague dramatic abstractions
- context-setting openings
- empty closing summaries
- repeated sentence length
- polite or frictionless dialogue
- translation-like Chinese
- perfectly balanced prose that nobody would actually write

## First Rule

Do not over-polish.

Useful roughness, asymmetry, silence, repetition, ambiguity, and awkwardness may carry voice. Keep them when they are intentional or character-bearing.

## Pass 1: Oral Test

Ask:

```text
Would a person with this narrator / POV / character voice actually phrase it this way?
```

If no, rewrite.

Prefer living connectors when they fit the voice:

- but
- so
- and then
- 不過
- 所以
- 結果
- 偏偏
- 只是

Cut filing-cabinet connectors unless the voice intentionally uses them:

- 此外
- 另外
- 值得注意的是
- 綜上所述
- 毋庸置疑
- in conclusion
- moreover
- furthermore
- it is worth noting

## Pass 2: Concrete Replacement

Replace abstract summary with visible evidence.

Weak:

```text
她感到非常悲傷。
```

Better direction:

```text
她把杯子放回桌上，手指沒有鬆開杯耳。
```

Do not explain the emotion if the scene can show it.

## Pass 3: Rhythm Break

Look for mechanical rhythm:

- three consecutive sentences of similar length
- repeated sentence openings
- repeated paragraph shape
- all dialogue lines having the same cadence
- every beat ending with an explanation

Fix by:

- inserting one short sentence
- letting a long sentence stretch forward
- cutting a summary line
- breaking one paragraph earlier than expected
- letting silence carry the reaction

## Pass 4: Generic Phrase Removal

Flag and rewrite generic lines like:

- 命運的齒輪開始轉動
- 一切都不再相同
- 他早已看穿一切
- 她不知道的是，真正的危機才正要開始
- 這將改變所有人的命運
- the world would never be the same
- little did they know
- a game-changing revelation

A dramatic sentence must carry a specific story fact, not just dramatic fog.

## Pass 5: Fiction-Specific Exception

Creative writing may intentionally use:

- repetition
- parallel structure
- fragment sentences
- silence
- stylized narration
- strange rhythm
- blunt statements

Keep these if they express POV, character, genre, or tension.

## Final Checklist

Before presenting prose, verify:

- [ ] No generic emotional summary where visible action would work better.
- [ ] No unexplained motivational-poster sentence.
- [ ] No empty context-setting opening.
- [ ] No empty closing summary.
- [ ] Rhythm is varied.
- [ ] Dialogue has friction.
- [ ] Chinese prose does not sound translated unless intentionally stylized.
- [ ] The author's roughness, ambiguity, and rhythm are preserved.

## Principle

Do not disinfect the prose. Remove plastic, not blood.
