# Failure Modes

## Purpose

This file lists common ways AI fiction fails. Use it during outline review, draft review, and revision.

The goal is not to punish the draft. The goal is to find the exact place where the story becomes fake, smooth, forgetful, inert, or decorative.

## Major Failure Modes

### 1. Outline Gate Failure

Symptoms:

- prose appears before the user approved the outline
- the agent assumes “interesting” means “approved”
- chapter drafting begins before continuity state is known

Fix:

- stop drafting
- produce outline
- ask for explicit approval

### 2. Continuity Drift

Symptoms:

- character knows information they never learned
- injury, debt, fear, guilt, or betrayal disappears
- object ownership changes without tracking
- travel, recovery, or communication becomes impossible
- payoff appears before setup

Fix:

- reconstruct story state
- update continuity files
- ask targeted questions if missing

### 3. Conflict Smoothing

Symptoms:

- characters calmly explain themselves
- conflict resolves as soon as someone apologizes
- everyone chooses the healthiest option
- the scene ends with no new pressure

Fix:

- add incompatible goals
- hide information
- add cost
- delay confession
- force a bad tradeoff

### 4. Lore Dump Opening

Symptoms:

- the scene opens with history, institutions, or world systems
- the character appears only after the worldbuilding lecture
- paragraphs could be pasted into a wiki

Fix:

- start from body, object, room, tool, vehicle, or action
- reveal systems through friction and consequence
- use close-to-wide movement

### 5. Decorative Science

Symptoms:

- numbers appear to sound smart
- equations do not change action
- explanation has no risk or decision
- technical terms are not tied to scene pressure

Fix:

- identify immediate problem
- add observation and estimate
- show uncertainty
- make the result change a decision

### 6. Fake Genius

Symptoms:

- “already saw through everything”
- no observation, test, or reasoning chain
- opponent becomes stupid
- plan requires perfect obedience or perfect timing

Fix:

- define domain, observation, options, tradeoff, cost, blind spot
- red-team the opponent
- include fallback or model update

### 7. Source-Cloning

Symptoms:

- exact or near-exact lines from a known source
- named setting, character roster, institutions, or plot beats are reproduced
- the result feels like a lightly renamed existing work

Fix:

- keep only broad craft technique
- invent new objects, pressures, institutions, and scene goals
- change POV, conflict, stakes, and causality

### 8. Dialogue-as-Wiki

Symptoms:

- characters tell each other facts they already know
- a line exists only to explain lore
- everyone speaks in the same assistant voice
- no intention or obstacle under the line

Fix:

- give each speaker a desire
- make the listener block it
- move information through accusation, deflection, threat, or mistake

### 9. Over-Polish

Symptoms:

- every sentence becomes elegant
- rough narrator texture disappears
- voice becomes neutral and safe
- emotional ambiguity is explained away

Fix:

- restore rhythm, bias, awkwardness, and silence
- preserve character-bearing imperfections
- remove plastic, not blood

### 10. Twist Washing Machine

Symptoms:

- too many major reversals stack without consequence
- characters have no time to react
- the story resets emotionally after each twist
- reveals exist only for shock

Fix:

- require setup, cost, permanent change, and consequence scene
- reduce twist density
- let characters pay for new knowledge

## Review Output

When using this file, report:

```markdown
| Failure mode | Location | Why it fails | Fix direction |
|---|---|---|---|
```

## Principle

A bad draft usually does not fail everywhere. Find the exact failure mode, then revise only what breaks the story.
