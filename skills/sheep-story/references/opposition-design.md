# Opposition Design

## Purpose

Use this reference when a person, group, institution, environment, relationship, or part of the viewpoint character persistently blocks the governing desire.

Opposition is not automatically a villain. It becomes dramatically useful when it can pursue something, impose pressure, adapt, and pay or transfer costs.

## Opposition Types

- **Person:** a rival, guardian, official, loved one, hunter, or competing protagonist
- **Group / institution:** a bureau, family, company, army, custom, market, or legal process
- **Environment / system:** weather, distance, scarcity, infrastructure, biology, or technology
- **Relationship:** incompatible needs, duties, promises, histories, or power
- **Internal:** fear, denial, compulsion, grief, loyalty, or a coping strategy that has become harmful

Combine types only when each changes the available choices. Do not invent a secret mastermind merely to give a system a face.

## Coherence Without Forced Redemption

Make the opposition's choices specific and locally rational. Coherence does not require sympathy, a tragic childhood, secret nobility, forgiveness, or redemption. Sympathy is optional; consequence and agency are not.

An antagonist can have a moving motive and still cause unacceptable harm. An institution can be coherent without sharing one consciousness. An environment needs consistent pressures and limits, not human motives.

## Red-Team Check

Before approving the outline, ask:

1. Does the opposition have independent action when the protagonist is absent?
2. Could it notice or counter the protagonist's obvious move?
3. Are its resources, information, constraints, and jurisdiction visible?
4. Does the conflict persist for a reason stronger than avoidable misunderstanding?
5. Does victory change the system of pressure rather than merely reveal that the opposition was foolish?

For deduction, tactics, or mastermind contests, also use `genius-strategy.md`.

## Principle

A strong opponent is not one the story excuses. It is one the story understands well enough to let act.
