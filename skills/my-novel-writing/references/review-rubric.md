# Review Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric makes story review stable and repeatable. Use it when reviewing outlines, chapters, scenes, or revisions.

Do not only say whether the writing is good. Identify which system is failing and what should be preserved.

## Scores

Use 1–5 scores.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Broken; blocks the scene from working |
| 2 | Weak; visible problem that needs revision |
| 3 | Functional; acceptable but could improve |
| 4 | Strong; minor improvements only |
| 5 | Excellent; preserve this behavior |

## Categories

Review these categories when relevant:

| Category | Checks |
|---|---|
| Continuity | Does it respect prior events, knowledge, objects, injuries, relationships, and timeline? |
| Conflict Pressure | Does every scene have desire, obstacle, cost, turn, and irreversible change? |
| Scene Texture | Does the world enter through body, object, action, friction, sensory detail, and desire? |
| Technical Explanation | Does science/math/engineering change action, decision, risk, or emotion? |
| Dialogue Subtext | Do lines have intention, obstacle, hidden meaning, and character-specific voice? |
| Character Agency | Do characters choose under pressure rather than get dragged by plot? |
| Genius Logic | Is intelligence shown through observation, reasoning, tradeoff, cost, and opponent competence? |
| Pacing | Do beats escalate, breathe, and pay consequences at the right density? |
| Anti-AI Flavour | Does it avoid generic summary, symmetry, plastic phrasing, and over-explanation? |
| Style Preservation | What roughness, rhythm, ambiguity, or voice must be preserved? |

## Review Format

Use this structure:

```markdown
## Review Summary

One-paragraph diagnosis.

## Scores

| Category | Score | Reason |
|---|---:|---|

## Critical Issues

Issues that must be fixed before drafting or continuing.

| Location | Issue | Why it breaks the story | Fix direction |
|---|---|---|---|

## Medium Issues

Issues that weaken the scene but do not block it.

## What To Preserve

List the parts that should not be smoothed away.

## Revision Plan

Ordered steps.

## Unresolved Questions

Only questions that block a good revision.
```

## What To Preserve Is Mandatory

Always include a preservation section when reviewing prose.

Good drafts often contain roughness worth keeping:

- a strange but effective line
- character-specific awkwardness
- silence
- repetition
- ugly directness
- emotional bias
- technical irritation
- dry humor

Do not erase the part that makes the writing alive.

## Principle

A review should not merely find flaws. It should protect what works while naming what breaks.
