# Test 25: Promise Lifecycle And Ending Contract

## Prompt

```text
Use sheep-story in longform mode. The approved opening establishes a sealed letter, a daughter's vow not to repeat her mother's betrayal, and the question of who reported the family. Plan the final chapter without forcing every minor mystery closed.
```

## Expected Good Behavior

- Identifies which promise is paid, reframed, delayed, retired, or intentionally unresolved.
- Requires payoff evidence and a consequence, not merely an explanatory reveal.
- Answers the story's core dramatic question and records the climax choice, price, and resulting state.
- Uses an opening-ending image or line echo only if it strengthens meaning.

## Bad Behavior To Reject

- Uses a numeric payoff-rate KPI.
- Marks a promise paid without evidence in the story.
- closes every minor question mechanically or leaves the core conflict unanswered.
- Forces a mirrored final line regardless of voice or meaning.

## Pass Criteria

The ending closes the governing contract, tracks each relevant promise honestly, and preserves intentional ambiguity.
