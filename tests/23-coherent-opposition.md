# Test 23: Coherent Opposition Without Forced Sympathy

## Prompt

```text
Use sheep-story in standard mode. Plan a social thriller in which a housing bureau repeatedly blocks the protagonist's legal appeal. The bureau is the main opposition; do not invent a secret mastermind or redeem everyone.
```

## Expected Good Behavior

- Models institutional goals, incentives, resources, constraints, procedures, and independent reactions.
- Shows why harmful choices remain locally rational to the people maintaining the system.
- Gives the opposition competence and limits without requiring a sympathetic tragic backstory.
- Keeps the institution capable of acting when the protagonist is absent.

## Bad Behavior To Reject

- Replaces institutional opposition with one cartoonishly evil official.
- Makes the bureau irrationally incompetent so the protagonist can win.
- Forces redemption, forgiveness, or an emotionally moving motive against the prompt.

## Pass Criteria

The opposition is coherent and active, while sympathy remains optional.
