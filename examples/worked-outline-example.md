# Worked Outline Example

This example shows the expected behavior of SheepStory before drafting prose.

## User Request

```text
Use sheep-story to plan a scene where a weak but brilliant exiled prince uses a poor rebel cell to win a local battle, but an unknown ace pilot breaks his plan.
Do not draft prose yet.
```

## Expected SheepStory Output

```markdown
## Proposed Outline

### Scene / Chapter Goal

Show that the prince is intelligent but not omniscient. He can convert bad resources into a local tactical advantage, but his model fails when an unknown battlefield anomaly appears.

### Current Continuity State

- Prince is physically weak but strategically gifted.
- Rebel cell is poorly trained, emotionally volatile, and disposable from a military perspective.
- Enemy command expects normal rebel tactics.
- Prince does not know the ace pilot exists.

### Beat 1 — Bad Resources, Useful Shape

- Purpose: Establish limited resources.
- POV: Prince.
- Desire: Win a local engagement without exposing himself.
- Obstacle: Rebels are undisciplined and under-equipped.
- Cost: He must treat frightened people as tactical pieces.
- Turn: He realizes their weakness can become bait.
- Irreversible change: He commits to using them, damaging his moral line.
- Subtext: He tells himself this is necessary.

### Beat 2 — Best Available Plan

- Purpose: Show constrained genius.
- Desire: Force enemy units into a kill corridor.
- Obstacle: He lacks elite troops and reliable communications.
- Cost: Several rebel units may be sacrificed.
- Turn: The enemy takes the bait because the plan exploits their contempt.
- Irreversible change: The prince wins local control but becomes responsible for deaths.
- Subtext: Victory tastes like evidence against himself.

### Beat 3 — Hidden Anomaly

- Purpose: Break the prince's model fairly.
- Desire: Finish the encirclement.
- Obstacle: An unknown ace pilot appears and moves outside expected reaction limits.
- Cost: The prince loses control of tempo.
- Turn: Two-unit and three-unit containment both fail.
- Irreversible change: The enemy now knows there is a hidden commander.
- Subtext: The prince experiences the first crack in his belief that people are predictable.

### Beat 4 — Damage Control

- Purpose: Let the genius fail intelligently.
- Desire: Escape without revealing identity.
- Obstacle: The ace pilot reaches his position.
- Cost: The prince must abandon the local victory.
- Turn: He chooses survival and information over pride.
- Irreversible change: The ace pilot becomes a named anomaly in future plans.
- Subtext: He hates that one person can invalidate a perfect model.

### Risks

- If the ace pilot appears with no setup, it may feel like author intrusion.
- If the prince loses too easily, he may look foolish.
- If the rebels are only cannon fodder, the scene may lose emotional texture.

### Questions for You

1. Should the prince feel guilt immediately, or suppress it until later?
2. Should the ace pilot recognize him, or only suspect a hidden commander?
3. Should the rebel cell survive as future allies, or fracture after being used?

Do you want to approve this outline for prose, or revise it first?
```

## Why This Passes

- No prose before approval.
- The prince is smart but not perfect.
- Bad resources become tactical value.
- The enemy does not become stupid.
- The ace pilot is a battlefield anomaly, not random author cheating.
- The failure updates the prince's future model.
